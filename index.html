▶ What is a birthday attack? ▼
A birthday attack is a real cryptographic exploit — used to crack MD5, SHA-1, and other hashing algorithms — named directly after this paradox.

The insight: you don't need to find your specific hash. You just need any two inputs that collide. With 365 possible birthdays, the math is:
Possible values (d): 365 birthdays
50% collision chance at: 23 people
99% collision chance at: 57 people
Expected first match: customer #23
Right now: 0 customers — 0.0% chance

This is the same math that let researchers forge SSL certificates using MD5 collisions in 2008 — and why NIST deprecated SHA-1 for code signing in 2016. We just compressed the proof into a coffee line.
