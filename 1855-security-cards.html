You just
witnessed
a live attack
With only 365 possible birthdays, a collision appeared in just a few customers. Hackers use the same math — the birthday attack — to crack password databases in minutes instead of decades.
5 tips to protect yourself right now
1
Use a password manager. Never reuse passwords — one breach exposes everything.
2
Enable 2FA everywhere. Even a stolen password is useless without your second factor.
3
Verify before you click. Phishing is the #1 attack vector — hover links before trusting them.
4
Public WiFi is an open room. Assume someone is listening. Use a VPN.
5
Update everything. 60% of breaches exploit vulnerabilities with patches already available.

The math
never lies —
hackers know it
A birthday attack cracked SSL certificates in 2008 using MD5. Your bank still asks for your birth month on security questions. The same math applies to both.
5 more things most people get wrong
1
Security questions are attackable. "Mother's maiden name" is often public. Use fake answers stored in your password manager.
2
"I have nothing to hide" is a myth. Your data has value — to advertisers, criminals, and nation-states.
3
SMS 2FA is better than nothing, but not great. SIM-swapping is real. Use an authenticator app.
4
Freeze your credit. It's free and makes identity theft dramatically harder.
5
Zero Trust, always. "Never trust, always verify" — online and in person.

Knowledge
is the best
firewall
94% of malware is delivered by email. 82% of breaches involve a human element. The weakest link in any security chain is the one that was never trained.
For your family & personal life
1
Talk to your kids about social engineering. Bad actors target children online first.
2
Check haveibeenpwned.com to see if your email appeared in a data breach.
3
Review app permissions monthly. Does your flashlight app need microphone access?
4
Back up the 3-2-1 way. 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite. Ransomware loses its leverage.
5
Security awareness training saves lives. Ask me about a free consultation.
